Resources

Helpful links, info, and campus resources.


About these resources

The resources below help navigate some of the writing, grammar, and research issues in CO150. I've also included some helpful campus links to other resources you may want to check out on campus. I may add to this list as the semester progresses.

Writing and Research Tools

Writing

  • CSU Writing Center
    Make an appointment with the CSU Writing Center—it's free! They offer one-on-one, 30 minute consultations at ANY stage of your writing process, whether you're just starting out or want input on your near-final draft.
  • Purdue Owl
    The Purdue Owl (Online Writing Lab) is a great resource for both grammar and writing tips.
  • Definitions of Rhetoric
    "Rhetoric" has been defined by several different scholars; here are a few compiled definitions to give you a better idea of this guiding course concept.
  • Definitions of Rhetoric
    "Rhetoric" has been defined by several different scholars; here are a few compiled definitions to give you a better idea of this guiding course concept.
  • Hemingway App
    Take the Hemingway App with several grains of salt, but it is a good tool for working on concise writing. This tool will also point out the passive voice.

Research

  • Morgan Library Research Guide
    Check out the CO150 research guide on our library's website.
  • Purdue Owl - MLA Guide
    MLA guide online from the Purdue Owl.
  • Printable MLA Guide
    MLA guide as a PDF. From the Joining the Conversation textbook. Contains the most commonly used works cited page citation examples.

Grammar & Mechanics

  • Purdue Owl Grammar / Mechanics
    A comprehensive topical discussion of MANY grammatical issues.
  • Purdue Owl - MLA Guide
    MLA guide online from the Purdue Owl.
  • Grammar Girl: Quick and Dirty Tips
    A blog/podcast about grammar that covers most common issue.
  • The Oatmeal
    Here's a link to all of The Oatmeal's (a webcomic) comics about grammar. Disclaimer: foul language and weird humor abounds.
